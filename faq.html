Q: How do I know if an email from TechNova is legitimate?

All official TechNova emails come from addresses ending in @technova.example. Be cautious of emails from similar-looking domains such as:

  • @tecknova.com (missing 'h')
  • @technnova.com (extra 'n')
  • @technova-systems.com (hyphenated)

We will never ask you to update your password or payment details via email.

Q: I received an email about CloudSafe Backup renewal. Is this real?

CloudSafe renewal notices come directly from @cloudsafe.com, not from TechNova. If you received a renewal notice claiming to be from CloudSafe but with a different domain, it may be fraudulent.

Legitimate CloudSafe emails will:

  • Come from noreply@cloudsafe.com
  • Include your account number
  • Never ask for passwords
  • Direct you to the official CloudSafe portal

Q: What should I do if I think I clicked on a suspicious link?

If you believe you may have clicked on a malicious link or entered your credentials on a suspicious website:

  1. Change your password immediately through our Staff Portal
  2. Contact IT Support at support@technova.example
  3. Do not enter any additional information on the suspicious site
  4. Report the incident to our security team

Q: How can I verify a TechNova employee?
