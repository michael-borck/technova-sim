üîç Investigation Tips

  • Start by exploring the public website - look for any notices or alerts
  • The press release might downplay the incident - read between the lines
  • Check the FAQ section - customers often ask about real problems
  • The staff portal might have "maintenance" messages with clues
  • Interview all four employees - they each know different pieces
  • Look for links to internal documents hidden in pages
  • Pay attention to email addresses and domain names
  • Compare any documents that seem similar (like invoices)
  • Note exact times when events occurred
  • Consider both technical AND human factors

‚ö†Ô∏è Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Don't ignore the "small" details - they often matter most
  • Don't trust everything at face value - verify information
  • Don't focus only on technology - humans are often the weak link
  • Don't rush - thorough investigation takes time
  • Don't forget to document your findings in the worksheet

Evidence Priority

Focus your investigation on finding:

  1. The initial attack vector - How did they get in?
  2. The compromised data - What was stolen or changed?
  3. The timeline - When did each event occur?
  4. The impact - Who was affected and how?
  5. The gaps - What security measures were missing?
